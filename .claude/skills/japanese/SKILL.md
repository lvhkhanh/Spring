# Japanese Learning and Customer Communication Skill

## Overview

This skill helps build practical Japanese ability for day-to-day project work with Japanese customers, especially for a Java solution architect or senior technical delivery role. It focuses on fast but sustainable improvement: small daily practice, useful business language, technical vocabulary, listening and speaking confidence, and communication that is professional without becoming stressful or unrealistic.

## Key Capabilities

### Daily Learning Plan
- Create short daily study routines that fit busy project schedules
- Balance speaking, listening, reading, writing, and vocabulary without overload
- Prioritize consistency over intensity for fast long-term improvement
- Adjust study tasks by available time such as 10, 20, or 30 minutes per day

### Business Communication
- Draft and improve Japanese emails, chat messages, meeting notes, and status updates
- Adjust tone for polite, standard business, or softer customer-facing communication
- Explain when to use direct wording versus more careful Japanese phrasing
- Improve naturalness while keeping messages simple and safe for non-native speakers

### Customer-Facing Project Language
- Prepare phrases for meetings, clarifications, risks, schedules, blockers, and follow-ups
- Practice requirement discussions, issue explanations, progress reporting, and dependency management
- Build confidence for introductions, small talk, and polite meeting transitions
- Support architecture and delivery conversations with Japanese customers and managers

### Technical Japanese for Java SA Work
- Learn useful vocabulary for Java, Spring, MyBatis, SQL, batch processing, APIs, design reviews, and migration projects
- Translate technical intent into Japanese that customers and offshore/onshore teams can understand
- Practice explaining design choices, tradeoffs, incidents, estimates, and action plans
- Rephrase overly advanced wording into practical business Japanese that is easier to remember and use

### Sustainable Progress and Motivation
- Reduce stress by breaking learning into repeatable, realistic steps
- Avoid perfectionism and optimize for “usable Japanese this week”
- Track progress through recurring patterns rather than large study goals
- Build review cycles that reinforce memory without making study feel heavy

## Common Patterns

### Low-stress daily routine
- 5 minutes: review yesterday's phrases or vocabulary
- 5 minutes: read one short business or technical sentence aloud
- 5 minutes: write one customer-facing message or status line
- 5 minutes: practice one speaking scenario for project work

### Meeting preparation pattern
- Prepare opening greeting and self-introduction
- Prepare 3 key points you need to communicate
- Prepare 3 clarification questions
- Prepare closing phrases and next-step confirmation

### Technical communication pattern
- State current status
- Explain issue or decision simply
- Give reason or impact
- Propose next action and timing

## Best Practices

- Keep Japanese simple, correct, and polite before trying to sound advanced
- Learn phrases in project context, not isolated vocabulary only
- Reuse a small number of high-frequency sentence patterns until they become natural
- Focus on listening and speaking for customer work, even if grammar is still imperfect
- Study every day in small sessions rather than pushing hard on weekends only
- Prefer practical business Japanese over textbook-only expressions
- Use shadowing, repetition, and rewrite exercises to improve speed without stress
- Build a personal phrase bank for meetings, emails, architecture reviews, and delivery updates

## Fast Improvement Strategy

### What to prioritize first
- Greetings, introductions, and meeting transitions
- Clarification questions and confirmation phrases
- Status, schedule, issue, risk, and action-item language
- Technical nouns and verbs you use repeatedly in Java and project work

### 4-week progression approach
- Week 1: survival phrases for meetings, greetings, and polite responses
- Week 2: project status updates, blockers, requests, and confirmations
- Week 3: technical explanations for design, API, batch, database, and migration topics
- Week 4: roleplay for real customer scenarios and message rewriting from your own project context

### Low-stress rules
- Do not try to memorize too many words at once
- Keep daily study small enough that you can still do it on busy days
- Accept partial speaking ability and improve through repetition
- Recycle the same useful patterns across email, chat, and meetings

## Troubleshooting

### Common learning issues
- **Too much grammar, not enough usage**: shift toward phrase practice and project-based examples
- **Can read but cannot speak**: add short daily speaking drills and roleplay
- **Can understand some words but not meetings**: practice listening to short business phrases with repetition and note-taking
- **Study feels stressful**: reduce time, narrow scope, and focus only on high-frequency customer scenarios

### Communication issues
- **Message sounds too direct**: soften requests and confirmations with more polite phrasing
- **Too formal to remember**: simplify to standard business Japanese that you can use consistently
- **Technical explanation is unclear**: split into status, cause, impact, and next action
- **Fear of mistakes in meetings**: prepare reusable sentence frames instead of full scripts only

### Validation Steps
1. Check whether the phrase is natural enough for business use.
2. Confirm that the meaning matches the intended project message.
3. Prefer wording you can remember and actually say under pressure.
4. Reuse the phrase in email, chat, and speaking practice the same week.
